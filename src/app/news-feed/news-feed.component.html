Health Care

Common Symptoms of Bone Cancer

  • Bone pain: Persistent or worsening pain in the affected bone.
  • Swelling and tenderness: Noticeable swelling near the affected area.
  • Weakened bone: Increased risk of fractures in affected bones.
  • Fatigue: Feeling constantly tired or weak.
  • Unintentional weight loss: Losing weight without trying.

Causes

The cause of most bone cancers isn't known. Bone cancer starts when cells in or near a bone develop changes in their DNA. A cell's DNA holds the instructions that tell the cell what to do. In healthy cells, the DNA gives instructions to grow and multiply at a set rate. The instructions tell the cells to die at a set time. In cancer cells, the DNA changes give different instructions. The changes tell the cancer cells to make many more cells quickly. Cancer cells can keep living when healthy cells would die. This causes too many cells. The cancer cells might form a mass called a tumor. The tumor can grow to invade and destroy healthy body tissue. In time, cancer cells can break away and spread to other parts of the body. When cancer spreads, it's called metastatic cancer.

Types of Bone Cancer

  • Osteosarcoma:
    • Most common type of bone cancer.
    • Starts in the cells that form bones.
    • Mostly affects teenagers and young adults, but can also occur in children and older adults.
    • Commonly found in long bones (legs and arms), but can rarely appear in soft tissue.
  • Chondrosarcoma:
    • Usually starts in the bones but can also occur in soft tissue.
    • Most common in the pelvis, hip, and shoulder.
    • Typically affects middle-aged and older adults.
  • Ewing Sarcoma:
    • Begins in bones or soft tissue around the bones.
    • Mostly affects children and young adults but can occur at any age.
    • Commonly starts in leg bones and pelvis but can appear in any bone.

Risk Factors

  • Inherited Genetic Syndromes: Certain rare genetic syndromes passed through families increase the risk of bone cancer. Examples include:
    • Li-Fraumeni syndrome
    • Hereditary retinoblastoma
  • Other Bone Conditions: Some conditions that may increase the risk of bone cancer include:
    • Paget's disease of bone
    • Fibrous dysplasia
  • Cancer Treatment: Radiation therapy for cancer and certain chemotherapy medicines may increase the risk of bone cancer.
